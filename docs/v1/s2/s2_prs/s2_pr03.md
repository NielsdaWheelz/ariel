# PR-03: Runtime Taint Provenance + Fail-Closed Side-Effect Authorization

## Goal

Close the Slice 2 trust gap by deriving side-effect taint from runtime provenance instead of model-declared flags, so untrusted content cannot silently authorize side effects.

## Builds On

PR-02 merged.

## Acceptance

- when a side-effecting proposal is materially influenced by tool-returned or external untrusted content, policy applies taint controls even if proposal taint metadata is missing, false, or malformed.
- side-effecting proposals never auto-execute based only on model-declared cleanliness; runtime-derived provenance governs authorization outcomes.
- deterministic fail-closed authorization is enforced for tainted or provenance-ambiguous proposals: `write_reversible` requires approval, while `write_irreversible` and `external_send` are denied.
- non-side-effecting `read` behavior remains bounded by existing allowlist/policy controls and does not clear taint for later side effects.
- action lifecycle events include provenance evidence and decision reasons sufficient to reconstruct why taint escalation or taint deny was applied.
- regression coverage proves side-effecting auto-execution cannot bypass taint controls through omitted, false, or malformed taint metadata.
- existing Slice 2 safety boundaries remain intact under provenance-driven taint decisions (integrity checks, egress constraints, guardrails, and serialized side-effect execution).

## Non-Goals

- does not add probabilistic trust scoring or adaptive trust heuristics.
- does not redesign cross-session memory trust policy.
- does not expand domain workflows or capability surface beyond framework-level safety behavior.
- does not deliver surface UX redesign for action lifecycle inspectability (PR-04).

## Key Decisions (if any beyond L2)

**Runtime provenance is the authorization source of truth**: model-provided taint hints are advisory only and cannot de-escalate or clear runtime-derived taint.

**Ambiguous provenance is treated as tainted and resolved by impact level**: for tainted/ambiguous cases, `write_reversible` escalates to approval and `write_irreversible`/`external_send` are denied.
